Lets clear something first

Do you know the difference between stamina and endurance, I feel it’s important to clear these two concepts. first, let’s look at what is endurance. Endurance is defined as how long an activity can be performed in total, although it is often used interchangeably with stamina, this two are not the same. Stamina refers to how long an activity can be performed at maximum capacity. Stamina considers your physical and mental burden of work whilst endurance refers to your cardiovascular efficiency. This is how well your heart, lungs and muscles work together to circulate blood and oxygen around your body to sustain activity. Activities that need to be sustained for long periods of time require endurance, activities like marathons. High stamina activities include soccer, tennis and basketball, there are others. These sports involve burst of high-intensity exercise followed by active recovery and going again. This is exhausting both physically and mentally.

Why Endurance

One of the best ways to improve your aerobic capacity is to build an endurance base. As your endurance increases, you speed naturally increases as well and adding more mileage to your run adds up to build higher stamina. Cross-country consists of troublesome terrains and on a good day, it could rain and make the track muddy and full of puddles. Endurance is key to finish these types of races, winning is another story on its own as cross-country can be very competitive. Having enough stamina without the endurance to compensate will give you enough the energy run a few kilometres, but you won’t be able to sustain it for a long period of time.
